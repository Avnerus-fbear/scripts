## Day 1 — Friday June 19: Turku City Exploration

### Morning: Old Town & Castle Area

- **Turku Castle** (Turun Linna) — Kids love castles! There's a family-friendly atmosphere, exhibitions, and the castle courtyard is great for running around. The castle restaurant is good for a late breakfast/brunch.
  - 📍 Luolavuorenkatu 7
  - ⏰ Open 10:00–18:00 (summer)
  - 💡 The 4yo will enjoy exploring the rooms; the 2yo can burn energy in the courtyard

- **Luostarinmäki Museum Village** — Historic wooden houses with craftspeople live at work (glassblowing, blacksmith, etc.). Very sensory and engaging for young kids. Lots of narrow streets to explore.
  - 📍 Luostarinmäki 3
  - 💡 Watch the glassblowing — toddlers find mesmerizing

- **Turku Market Square** (Kauppatori) — Fresh produce, street food, ice cream. Let the kids pick something. Great atmosphere in summer.
  - 📍 Aurakatu 1
  - 💡 Grab fishcakes or ice cream from the stalls

### Lunch

- **Turun Kauppahalli** (Turku Market Hall) — Indoor food market with multiple vendors. S Wallin's fish shop is legendary. Plenty of options for picky eaters.
  - 📍 Rauhankatu 2

### Afternoon: River & Islands

- **Sörnäinen Island** — A recreational island connected by bridge. Has a sandy beach, playground, and shallow water perfect for a 2yo. Very relaxed, family-friendly.
  - 📍 Sörnäistenkatu 15
  - 💡 Bring swimsuits! The shallow beach is ideal for toddlers

- **Föri Ferry** — The free ferry crossing the Aura River. Kids love boats! It runs continuously and is completely free.
  - 📍 Departs from Market Square side
  - 💡 A quick, fun experience that costs nothing

- **Botanical Garden** (Yliopiston kasvitieteellinen puutarha) — Beautiful gardens with a tropical greenhouse. The tropical house is magical for little ones — exotic plants, humid air, colorful flowers.
  - 📍 Lemminkäisenkatu 22
  - 💡 The greenhouse is the highlight for toddlers

### Evening

- **Aurachannel promenade** walk — Beautiful summer evening, street performers sometimes around
- Dinner in Old Town area — several family-friendly restaurants near the castle

## Day 2 — Saturday June 20: Muumimaailma (Moominworld) 🌟

**This is THE day.** Midsummer celebration at the park!

### Getting There

- **By car:** ~20 min from Turku center (16 km)
- **By boat:** There's a summer boat connection from Turku — very scenic and fun for kids
- **By bus:** Föli bus from Turku
- 📍 Kailon saari, Naantali

### ⭐ Midsummer Special (June 19-20)

You're visiting during **Moomin Family's Midsummer Dance** — extra special!
- **2:00 PM** — Grand Midsummer Dance at Moominhouse (communal singing & dancing)
- Special Midsummer ice cream (€7)
- Pancakes with jam, strawberries, whipped cream & ice cream (€7.90/€6.50)
- Free Snorkmaiden paw decoration with face painting purchase

### Must-See Attractions (Toddler-Tested)

**🏠 Moominhouse** — The iconic house! Kids can explore from the attic to the jam cellar. The 4yo will love this.

**🎭 Theatre Emma** — Main show "Snorkmaiden presents: Grande Cabaré"
- Performances at 12:00, 13:00, 14:00, 15:00, 16:00
- Available in Finnish, Swedish, and English
- ⏱ ~20 min show, good enough for a 4yo

**🎪 Small Stage** — Multiple short shows throughout the day
- 11:30 — Little My's Morning Mischief (Finnish)
- 12:30/14:30/15:30 — Jam Adventure
- 13:30 — Moomin Family Singing Moment (music, no language barrier!)
- Perfect for short attention spans

**🤖 Snork's Park of Inventions** — Puppet theatre with "Snork's Space Adventure"
- Showings at 12:00, 13:00, 15:00, 16:00
- Great for the 4yo

**🌸 Alice's Herb Garden** — "Alice's Playtime" at 12:00, 13:00, 15:30
- Perfect for the 2yo — gentle, interactive play

**🎵 Snufkin's Camp** — Storytelling at 13:45, 14:45, 15:45
- Available in Finnish and Swedish

**🏖 Beach** — Yes, there's a beach at Moominworld! Bring swimsuits.
- 💡 The 2yo will love splashing in the shallow water

**🎨 Snorkmaiden's Beauty Salon** — Face painting (with special Midsummer paw decoration)

**🎠 Playground areas** — Multiple play areas scattered throughout the island

### Tips for Toddlers

- **Arrive early** (opens 10:00) to beat crowds and see shows first run
- **Magic bracelet** at entrance — good for keeping track of kids
- **Stroller-friendly** paths throughout the island
- **Restaurant** on-site with kids' menu
- **Sniff's Shop** for souvenirs
- **Pappan's Foto** — professional family photos with Moomin characters
- Ticket from ~€30-43, book online for best price

### Afternoon/Evening

- After Moominworld, explore **Naantali Old Town** — beautiful cobblestone streets, colorful wooden houses
- **Naantali beach** — one of Finland's most famous beaches, great for evening play
- Dinner in Naantali — **Merisali** restaurant recommended for fresh seafood

## Day 3 — Sunday June 21: Turku Return & More Fun

### Morning: Active Play

- **Seikkis (Adventure Park)** — Turku's best playground! Huge adventure park at Kupittaa with:
  - Mushroom shower (water feature) 🚿
  - Paddling pool for the 2yo
  - Climbing structures for the 4yo
  - Sand areas
  - Completely FREE
  - 📍 Kupittaa Park area
  - 💡 This is a MUST — best playground in Turku for toddlers

- **Kupittaa Bird Sanctuary** — Right next to Seikkis. Kids can see birds up close. Very peaceful.

### Late Morning

- **Forum Marinum** — Maritime museum with interactive exhibits. Has a large boat collection and hands-on displays. The 4yo will enjoy the interactive parts.
  - 📍 Aurakatu 144
  - 💡 More engaging for the 4yo; 2yo might prefer the outdoor areas

### Lunch

- **Sörnäinen** area has good food options, or head back to Market Square

### Afternoon: More Exploration

- **Turku Cathedral** — Currently under renovation (until Christmas 2028), but the exterior and 101m tower are impressive. Quick photo stop.
  - 📍 Tuomiokirkonkatu 9

- **Aboa Vetus Ars Nova** — Archaeology museum built on top of ancient ruins. You can see the actual medieval foundations through glass floors. Fascinating concept but might be more adult-focused. Skip if kids are tired.
  - 📍 Vapaudenkatu 1–3

- **Turku City Library** — Beautiful building with a dedicated children's section. Great rainy-day backup.
  - 📍 Linnanmaa 6

### Final Afternoon

- **Sörnäinen beach** return visit or **Lastenlinna** (Children's Castle) playground if time allows
- **Ice cream** at the Market Square — a proper trip finale 🍦

## Practical Tips

### For the 2yo
- Stroller is very useful, especially at Moominworld
- Nap-friendly: Seikkis has quiet corners, Moominworld has peaceful spots
- Beach/shallow water will be the biggest hit
- Alice's Herb Garden playtime is specifically toddler-friendly

### For the 4yo
- Can handle most shows and attractions
- The castle, glassblowing, and Moomin shows will be highlights
- Can participate in outdoor games at Moominworld

### General
- **Weather:** Late June in Turku = 15-22°C, long daylight (sunset ~10 PM). Pack layers.
- **Insect repellent** — midges can be active in June
- **Swimsuits** for both kids (beach + Moominworld)
- **Sunscreen** — Finnish sun is stronger than you'd expect
- **Cash + card** — most places take cards, but some market stalls prefer cash
- **Föli app** for public transport if not driving

### Packing Checklist
- [ ] Swimsuits (x2) + towels
- [ ] Sunscreen + hats
- [ ] Insect repellent
- [ ] Stroller (collapsible)
- [ ] Change of clothes (x2 per kid, for beach/water)
- [ ] Snacks + water bottles
- [ ] Small first aid (plasters etc.)
- [ ] Camera/phone for Moominworld photos
